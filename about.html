About Us

Doxa Health and Wellness LLC

Doxa Health and Wellness LLC provides comprehensive psychiatric services via Telehealth platform.

We are a private practice made up of a team of providers committed to ensuring the highest quality mental health services in the state of Maryland.

Telehealth services provide a rapid response to your treatment. We provide medication management for a variety of mental health conditions, including ADHD/ADD, anxiety, depression, schizophrenia, mood disorders, and more. We diagnose and treat children, adolescents, and adults.

Working with Doxa means working to find the right fit with one of our providers, all of whom are board certified Psychiatric Nurse Practitioners dedicated to improving your mental well-being. Our providers bring with them a wealth of knowledge and experience. Each are committed to working with you in the way that works best for you.

Mission 

To enhance the quality of life of individuals dealing with mental health challenges by providing consistent, high quality psychiatric care services.

Vision

We hope to meet the needs of individuals in Maryland and other parts of the United States, dealing with various mental health conditions.

Andinwe Namanga, CRNP-PMH

Andinwe (Andin) is a board-certified psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner with over 10 years experience in health care. She is licensed to provide psychiatric evaluation and treatment in Maryland. During the past decade, she has been caring for patients with a wide variety of mental health conditions. Andin’s experience includes caring for patients in outpatient settings and inpatient acute psychiatric units.

Andin graduated from Walden University in 2020. She is experienced in providing psychiatric care to children, adolescents and adults. She treats a variety of psychiatric conditions such as anxiety, depression, ADHD, schizophrenia and bipolar disorder.

Andin describes herself as a person who goes above and beyond to address her patient’s needs. She cares for her patient’s overall well-being and is always willing to collaborate with the patient and other providers to deliver the safest and most effective treatment. Andin has been an active member of the American Psychiatric Nurses Association since 2013. She is a strong advocate for the destigmatization of mental illness.

On a personal note, Andin is married and is the mom of three beautiful children. She believes her faith in God has helped her remain grounded and has played a big part in who she is.

Andin strives to provide patients with the best experience using a genuine caring approach. You will find that Andin is patient, understanding and very knowledgeable.

Fulgentia Smith, MPH, RN, BSN

Fulgentia is a licensed registered nurse with several years of experience in the medical field, ranging from working in hospital acute care settings, to out-patient and long-term care settings. Her strong passion for helping the sick pushed her to pursue a graduate degree in public health; and subsequently into exploring the correlations between individual mental health challenges and broader health events such as the Coronavirus pandemic.

Fulgentia finds the added dimension of understanding the mind beyond assessing physical ailments alone, not only an interesting but necessary part of a holistic health approach. She finds that there is a great deal of need for mental health practitioners in our community. Additionally, Fulgentia is commuted to removing the stigma associated with seeking treatment. She would like to make mental health more accessible to more people and feels that Doxa’s platform helps to do exactly that.

Josephine Gang, RN, BSN

Josephine has been a nurse for over a decade and was an educator before that. She has years of experience in various psychiatric settings. She has worked with a broad array of people, all presenting with their own diverse set of challenges. She has years of experience caring for individuals with various psychiatric conditions both in the inpatient and outpatient setting. She understands the importance of providing care to not just individual but to their families and the community as a whole.

She strives to improve the quality of life for others and emphasizes strong social and communal support systems as a critical part of a comprehensive mental health plan.

On a personal note, Josephine is the mother of 3 children, all of whom are in medical pursuits. Josephine shares their passion for mental health. She loves to travel and spend time with her family and friends.
